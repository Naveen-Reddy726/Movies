BREAKING BAD

Breaking Bad is an acclaimed American neo-Western crime drama television series that follows the transformation of a mild-mannered
high school chemistry teacher into a ruthless drug kingpin. The show is widely considered one of the greatest TV series of all time.

Premise: Walter White, an over-qualified and dispirited chemistry teacher, is diagnosed with terminal lung cancer.
Desperate to secure his family's financial future, he partners with a former student, Jesse Pinkman, to manufacture
and sell high-purity methamphetamine. Setting: The series is set and was filmed in Albuquerque,
New Mexico, running for five seasons from January 20, 2008, to September 29, 2013, on the AMC network.
Critical Acclaim: It has received numerous awards, including 16 Primetime Emmy Awards,
and held the Guinness World Record for the most critically acclaimed TV show of all time. The series is praised for its meticulous writing,
complex character development, and compelling story arcs.
